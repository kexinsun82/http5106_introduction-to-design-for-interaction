Intro Design for Interaction - IXD-5106-0NA

Activity: "UX Detective" Solve the Mystery of a Bad Interface!

Objective: Students will play the role of "UX detectives," investigating a real-world interface to identify usability issues and recommend fun, creative fixes based on UCD and IXD principles.


Duration: 3040 minutes


Materials Needed:

Access to a website or app that has some known usability flaws (e.g., an older or unpopular website or a quirky mobile game)

"Detective Notebooks" (simple handouts with prompts for observations)

Sticky notes or markers for brainstorming improvements

Instructions:

The Case of the Tricky Interface (5 minutes): Introduce students to the concept of UCD and IXD by explaining that they are UX detectives tasked with solving the mystery of a "tricky" or frustrating interface. Their goal is to investigate the interface and figure out why its difficult or confusing for users.

Use some fun detective metaphors (e.g., "Your mission, detectives, is to crack the case of The Disappearing Button or The Endless Scrolling Maze!").


Explore the Clues (1015 minutes): Students are divided into small detective teams. Each team is assigned an interface (website or app) and given a "Detective Notebook" with the following prompts:

"What's the Clue?" (What usability issue did you find? For example, confusing navigation, lack of feedback, too many clicks, etc.)

"Whos the Suspect?" (What part of the interface is responsible for the issue? A button? A menu? A confusing message?)

"Whats Your Evidence?" (Why do you think this would confuse or frustrate a user?)

"Your Solution?" (Come up with a fun way to solve the problem using UCD and IXD principles!)

Students can use sticky notes to mark problem areas and brainstorm creative solutions.


Present Your Case (10 minutes): Each team presents their findings to the class, sharing the usability issues they identified and their creative solutions. Encourage them to have fun with it, maybe even act like detectives presenting evidence in a quirky way.
